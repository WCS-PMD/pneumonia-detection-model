What is Pnuemonia??

Get to Know: Causes, Symptoms, and the Differences!

Normal Lungs

This image shows a set of noraml lungs. When looking at the other images its clear to see the differences between a pair of healthy lungs and ones plaqued with pneumonia. Some things you can do to keep your lungs in good condition are to:

Not smoke, cigarettes or vapes (also good to avoid second hand smoke), exercise regulary, a diet that has lots of fruits and veggies, drinking lots of water, and by practicing deep breathing!

Bacterial Pneumonia

This image shows lungs affected by bacterial pneumonia, characterized by infiltration and inflammation.

Bacterial Pneumonia can invovle one section of your lung, or attack your entire lung Some symptoms include: coughing with thick discoloured mucus, chest pain, fever, headaches severe fatigue and muscle pain. If you have trouble breathing, find blood in your mucus, a rapid heartbeat or a fever at 102.5°F or higher(39.1°C) talk to a doctor right away!

Viral Pneumonia

Here, you can see lungs affected by viral pneumonia, typically presenting with diffuse bilateral infiltrates. Viral pneumonia, develops from viral infection in the lungs. Can be caused by a vast amount of viruses, including: influenza, varicella-zoster virus , and coronaviruses.

Symptoms of viral pneumonia include: fevers, dry coughs that develops into one with mucus, shaking, shortness of breath fatigue, and weakness to name a few.
